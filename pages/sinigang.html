SINIGANG

Description

Sinigang is a Filipino soup or stew characterized by its sour and savory taste. It is most often associated with tamarind (sampalok), although it can use other sour fruits and leaves as the souring agent. It is one of the popular viands in Philippine cuisine. Other versions of the dish derive their sourness from native fruits like calamansi, guava, or santol. Others may use vinegar, unripe mango, or other ingredients. It is one of the most popular and traditional Filipino dishes, and is seen by some as the Philippine national dish.


Ingredients

  • 1 Pork Tenderloin
  • 1 Pork Liver
  • 1 Pork Kidney
  • 4 thumbs of ginger
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1 onion
  • 1 garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
  • 3 pieces of laurel leaves
  • 1 Knorr Pork Cube
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 green pepper
  • Ground black pepper and salt to taste

Instructions

  1. In a pot, heat the oil and saut√© the ginger, onion, and garlic.
  2. Add the pork and cook until browned.
  3. Add the soy sauce, vinegar, laurel leaves, and Knorr Pork Cube. Stir well.
  4. Add water and bring to a boil. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. Add the vegetables and cook until tender.
  6. Season with ground black pepper and salt to taste.
